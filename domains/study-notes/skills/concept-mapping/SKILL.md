# Concept Mapping

## Steps
1. Identify the central topic or question and place it at the center
2. Brainstorm all related concepts, terms, and subtopics
3. Group related concepts and arrange them in clusters around the center
4. Draw connections between concepts with labeled arrows (e.g., "causes", "depends on", "is a type of")
5. Add hierarchical structure: general concepts at top, specific details at bottom
6. Review for completeness: are there missing connections? Any isolated concepts?

## Examples
- Biology: central topic "Photosynthesis" → connected to "Chloroplast", "Light Reactions", "Calvin Cycle", "ATP", "NADPH"
- Programming: central topic "REST API" → connected to "HTTP Methods", "Endpoints", "Authentication", "JSON"
- History: central topic "Cold War" → connected to "NATO", "Warsaw Pact", "Proxy Wars", "Nuclear Arms Race"

## Anti-patterns
- Too many concepts without clear hierarchy (becomes a spiderweb)
- Unlabeled connections (the user doesn't know the relationship type)
- Creating the map after studying instead of using it as a learning tool
- Including irrelevant details that clutter the map
