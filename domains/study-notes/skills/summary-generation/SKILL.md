# Summary Generation

## Steps
1. Read source material thoroughly and identify the central thesis or main argument
2. Extract key supporting points (typically 3-7 main ideas)
3. Eliminate examples, anecdotes, and secondary details unless essential
4. Paraphrase in your own words — don't just copy sentences
5. Preserve the logical flow and structure of the original argument
6. Aim for 10-25% of the original length (longer for complex material, shorter for simple)
7. Include a final "takeaway" sentence encapsulating the core message

## Examples
- Research paper → one-page summary: research question, methodology, key findings, limitations
- Textbook chapter → outline: key definitions, theorems, formulas, worked examples
- Article → 3-bullet summary with action items

## Anti-patterns
- Summary longer than the original (defeats purpose)
- Copying sentences verbatim without understanding (plagiarism + no learning)
- Including minor details at the expense of main ideas
- Summarizing without adding your own structure or organization
