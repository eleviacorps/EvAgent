# Narrative Crafting

## Steps
1. Define the protagonist: the reader, a customer, or a character the audience relates to
2. Establish stakes: what does the protagonist stand to gain or lose?
3. Structure with narrative arc: exposition → rising action → climax → falling action → resolution
4. Use concrete details and sensory language to make the story vivid
5. Include conflict or tension: the problem that needs solving, the obstacle to overcome
6. End with transformation: how did the protagonist change? What was learned or achieved?

## Examples
- Case study: customer had problem X (conflict) → used product Y (climax) → achieved results Z (resolution)
- Origin story: founder identified gap (exposition) → built solution against odds (rising action) → launched successfully (climax)
- Thought leadership: industry trend creates challenge (tension) → new approach provides answer (resolution)

## Anti-patterns
- Starting with background instead of hooking the reader immediately
- Telling instead of showing ("the product is great" vs "revenue grew 300%")
- Making the story about you instead of the reader/customer
- No clear takeaway or call to action
