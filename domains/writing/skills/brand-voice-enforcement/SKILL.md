# Brand Voice Enforcement

## Steps
1. Extract brand voice attributes: formal vs casual, authoritative vs friendly, technical vs accessible
2. Build a voice reference: sample phrases, approved vocabulary, words to avoid
3. Check each piece against voice attributes — every sentence should reflect the brand
4. Review vocabulary: does word choice match the brand lexicon?
5. Check sentence structure: short and punchy vs long and elegant vs somewhere between
6. Verify consistency across all outputs for the same brand

## Examples
- Technical B2B: precise terminology, data-driven, third-person, formal
- Consumer lifestyle: conversational, inclusive, first-person, aspirational
- Developer tools: direct, jargon-appropriate, brief, no fluff

## Anti-patterns
- Mixing tones (calling someone "dude" in an otherwise formal document)
- Trying to appeal to everyone (brand voice that sounds generic)
- Copying competitor voice instead of defining your own
- Ignoring brand voice for "creative" pieces
