# Editing and Self-Review

## Steps
1. Take a break — distance yourself from the text for fresh eyes
2. Read aloud — catch awkward phrasing, run-on sentences, missing words
3. Check macro structure: does the argument flow? Are sections in the right order?
4. Check paragraphs: one idea per paragraph, clear topic sentences, logical transitions
5. Check sentences: cut passive voice, remove weasel words, tighten phrasing
6. Check word choice: remove jargon for general audiences, ensure terminology precision
7. Proofread: spelling, grammar, punctuation, formatting consistency

## Examples
- Before: "It is important to note that the implementation of the feature was done by the engineering team."
- After: "The engineering team implemented the feature."
- Before: "There are several different options available that users can choose from."
- After: "Users can choose from several options."

## Anti-patterns
- Editing while writing (write first, edit later — separate passes)
- Over-editing (removing all personality from the text)
- Relying only on spell-check (it won't catch "their/there/they're")
- Editing only at the sentence level without checking overall structure
