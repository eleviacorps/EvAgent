# Tone Calibration

## Steps
1. Identify the communication context: purpose, audience relationship, channel norms
2. Choose tone dimensions: formal ↔ casual, assertive ↔ polite, emotional ↔ factual, urgent ↔ measured
3. Adjust vocabulary: formal vocabulary for professional contexts, conversational for casual
4. Adjust sentence structure: shorter sentences for urgency/directness, longer for nuance/politeness
5. Check salutation and closing: "Dear Sir/Madam" vs "Hey" / "Sincerely" vs "Cheers"
6. Review for cultural sensitivity and regional language norms

## Examples
- Client proposal: formal, confident, data-backed ("We recommend proceeding with...")
- Team Slack: casual, direct ("Quick question — can we push this to tomorrow?")
- Customer complaint: empathetic, solution-oriented ("I understand this is frustrating. Here's how we'll fix it.")
- Celebratory: enthusiastic, personal ("Thrilled to share that we hit our Q2 target! 🎉")

## Anti-patterns
- Using the same tone for all audiences (one-size-fits-none)
- Over-formality that creates distance ("I am writing to inform you" → "Just letting you know")
- Casual tone in serious contexts (legal, compliance, condolences)
- Sarcasm or humor that doesn't translate across cultures
