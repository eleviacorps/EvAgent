# Platform Adaptation

## Steps
1. Identify platform norms: character limits, media support, posting frequency, content format
2. Adapt length: Twitter/X (280 chars), LinkedIn posts (1,300-2,000 chars), email (any length)
3. Adjust tone per platform: professional on LinkedIn, conversational on Twitter, personal on Instagram
4. Optimize media: image ratios (1:1 for Instagram, 16:9 for YouTube), video length (60s TikTok, 2min LinkedIn)
5. Use platform-native features: hashtags (Instagram/Twitter), threads (Twitter), articles (LinkedIn)
6. Schedule for platform peak times: LinkedIn weekdays 8-10am, Instagram evenings, Twitter continuous

## Examples
- Blog post → LinkedIn article: professional tone, intro paragraph as hook, bullet points for skimmability
- Blog post → Twitter thread: key takeaway in first tweet, each tweet covers one point, numbered thread
- Announcement → Email: structured with subject line, greeting, body, CTA, signature
- Announcement → Slack: brief, @channel or @here, link to details, emoji for visibility

## Anti-patterns
- Posting identical content across all platforms (ignores platform culture)
- Writing LinkedIn content that's too casual, or Twitter content that's too formal
- Over-hashtagging (Instagram up to 30, LinkedIn best with 3-5, avoid on Facebook)
- Posting at wrong times when audience is inactive
