# Engagement Optimization

## Steps
1. Craft compelling hooks: first sentence must grab attention (question, statistic, bold statement)
2. Use clear calls to action: specific, actionable, low-friction ("Comment your thoughts" not "Engage")
3. Structure for scanability: short paragraphs, bullet points, bold key phrases, white space
4. Add multimedia: images increase engagement 2x, video 5x, infographics for data-heavy content
5. Include interactive elements: polls, questions, fill-in-the-blank, "agree/disagree"
6. Time posts for peak audience activity based on platform analytics
7. Engage back: reply to comments, acknowledge responses, continue the conversation

## Examples
- Hook: "I tried 10 productivity apps so you don't have to. Here are the 3 that actually work. 🧵"
- CTA: "Tag someone who needs to see this" / "Drop your biggest challenge in the comments"
- Email subject line: "Your [Product] report is ready (plus a surprise inside)"
- LinkedIn: end with "What would you add?" to invite replies

## Anti-patterns
- Clickbait that doesn't deliver (hurts trust)
- Asking for engagement without giving value first
- Generic CTAs ("Like and share") without a reason to
- Neglecting to respond to comments after asking for them
