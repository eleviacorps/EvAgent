# Crisis Communication

## Steps
1. Acknowledge the situation promptly — silence is perceived as indifference
2. Express empathy: validate affected stakeholders' feelings without defensiveness
3. Communicate facts transparently: what happened, what's known, what's under investigation
4. Take responsibility where appropriate, avoid blame-shifting or vague language
5. Outline concrete actions: what's being done to address the situation, timeline, next updates
6. Maintain consistent messaging across all channels and spokespeople
7. Follow up when new information is available; close the loop publicly

## Examples
- Service outage: "We're aware of the outage affecting [X]. Our team is investigating. Next update in 30 min."
- Product recall: "Customer safety is our priority. We've identified an issue with [product] and are issuing voluntary recalls. Here's how to get a refund/replacement."
- Controversial statement: "We hear you. Our recent post was insensitive. We're sorry. Here's what we're doing to do better."

## Anti-patterns
- Delaying response to "gather more information" (creates information vacuum)
- Defensive or legal-sounding language that lacks empathy
- Making promises you can't keep (over-committing on timelines or outcomes)
- Deleting comments or complaints (erodes trust further)
- Using different messaging on different channels (creates confusion)
