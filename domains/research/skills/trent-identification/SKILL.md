# Trend Identification

## Steps
1. Cast a wide net — monitor diverse sources: academic, industry reports, news, social media, patents
2. Identify signals: new terminology, startup funding, regulatory changes, patent filings, hiring patterns
3. Assess momentum: is interest growing (search volume, publication rate, conference sessions)?
4. Categorize trends: hype cycle phase (innovation trigger → peak → trough → plateau)
5. Distinguish fads from genuine shifts: does the trend have staying power, infrastructure, and utility?
6. Project impact: who will be affected, how, and over what timeframe?

## Examples
- AI trend detection: rising GitHub stars for LLM tools → VC funding pattern → enterprise adoption curves
- Social trend: tracking hashtag growth, sentiment shifts, and mainstream media coverage over time
- Technology S-curve: compare early vs mature adoption indicators for a given innovation

## Anti-patterns
- Over-indexing on hype cycle peaks (assuming every trend is permanent)
- Confusing niche enthusiast interest with mainstream adoption
- Recency bias — mistaking what's new for what's important
- Ignoring counter-signals and disconfirming evidence
