# Research Synthesis

## Steps
1. Extract key claims and evidence from each source
2. Identify areas of consensus, disagreement, and uncertainty across sources
3. Group findings into themes or dimensions (e.g. technological, economic, social impacts)
4. Weigh evidence quality: meta-analyses > RCTs > observational studies > expert opinion > anecdotes
5. Construct a narrative that presents the landscape, key debates, and open questions
6. Produce structured output: executive summary, findings, methodology, limitations, recommendations

## Examples
- Competitive landscape: market size → key players → feature comparison → moats → emerging threats
- Technology assessment: capability overview → maturity level → adoption curve → key players → risks
- Policy analysis: problem context → stakeholder positions → proposed solutions → trade-offs

## Anti-patterns
- Just listing source summaries without synthesis
- Cherry-picking evidence that supports a preconceived conclusion
- Ignoring contradictory evidence or weak methodological sources
- Overclaiming certainty given the evidence base
