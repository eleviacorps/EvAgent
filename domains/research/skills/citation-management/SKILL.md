# Citation Management

## Steps
1. Capture full metadata: authors, title, publication, date, DOI/URL, publisher, volume/issue/pages
2. Choose citation style: APA (social sciences), MLA (humanities), Chicago (history), IEEE (engineering)
3. Format in-text citations consistently: (Author, Year) or [1] or Author (Year)
4. Build bibliography: alphabetical or order-of-appearance, consistent formatting
5. Link to DOI or stable URL whenever possible
6. Verify all DOIs resolve and URLs are still live

## Examples
- APA: (Smith, 2020, p. 45) in-text; Smith, J. (2020). *Title*. Publisher.
- IEEE: [1] in-text; [1] J. Smith, *Title*, Publisher, 2020.
- Use tools: Zotero, Mendeley, BibTeX for automated formatting

## Anti-patterns
- Inconsistent style within a single document
- Missing DOIs or URLs when available
- Citing a secondary source without acknowledging it (citing a quote from paper B that's in paper A)
- Over-citation (citing 20 sources for a basic, uncontroversial claim)
