# Fundamental Analysis

## Steps
1. Analyze financial statements: income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement
2. Calculate key ratios: P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, debt-to-equity, ROE, free cash flow yield
3. Assess competitive moat: brand, patents, network effects, switching costs, cost advantages
4. Evaluate management: capital allocation track record, insider ownership, incentive alignment
5. Analyze macro environment: interest rates, inflation, sector trends, regulatory landscape
6. Perform valuation: DCF, comparable company analysis, precedent transactions

## Examples
- Value investing: low P/E + high dividend yield + strong balance sheet + competitive moat
- Growth investing: high revenue growth + expanding margins + large TAM + strong unit economics
- Macro trade: rising rates → overweight financials, underweight growth/tech

## Anti-patterns
- Focusing only on the income statement (cash flow and balance sheet matter too)
- Using forward P/E without questioning the growth assumptions
- Ignoring debt levels (a company with high debt is fragile in rising rate environments)
- Confusing a good company with a good stock (price already reflects the quality)
