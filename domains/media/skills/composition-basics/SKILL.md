# Composition Basics — Visual Framing

## Steps
1. Apply rule of thirds: place key elements along grid lines or at intersection points
2. Lead with lines: use natural lines (roads, buildings, arms) to guide the eye to the subject
3. Frame within frame: use foreground elements (windows, arches) to frame the subject
4. Manage depth: separate foreground, midground, background for 3D feel
5. Balance visual weight: distribute elements evenly, or create intentional asymmetry
6. Leave headroom and lead room: appropriate space above the head and in front of the subject's gaze
7. Choose focal length: wide (context/environment), normal (natural), telephoto (compression/intimacy)

## Examples
- Interview: subject on left third, looking toward right, eyes on upper third line
- Product shot: centered with negative space on sides for text overlay
- Landscape: horizon on lower third for sky emphasis, upper third for ground emphasis
- Motion title: text balanced with graphic elements, aligned to grid

## Anti-patterns
- Centering everything (static, boring composition)
- Cutting off joints in framing (ankles, wrists, knees — crop mid-limb instead)
- Too much headroom (subject appears small/lost)
- Ignoring the background (distracting elements behind the subject)
