# Color Grading

## Steps
1. Perform primary color correction: fix white balance, exposure, contrast, and saturation
2. Apply color theory: complementary (blue/orange), analogous (blue/teal/green), or monochromatic
3. Create mood with color palette: warm (comfort/nostalgia), cool (sadness/technology), desaturated (gritty)
4. Use secondary grading: isolate and adjust specific colors (skin tones, skies, product colors)
5. Apply power windows: track masks to adjust specific regions of the frame
6. Add filmic look: slight S-curve for contrast, grain, halation, or LUT for specific emulation
7. Ensure shot-to-shot consistency across the entire project

## Examples
- Corporate video: clean, natural skin tones, brand colors (blue + white), slightly warm
- Action: teal/orange look (cool shadows, warm skin), high contrast, crushed blacks
- Retro 80s: magenta/cyan palette, halation, film grain, lifted blacks
- Documentary: neutral, true-to-life color with minimal grading

## Anti-patterns
- Over-grading (skin tones look plastic, unnatural saturation)
- Crushing blacks to hide underexposure (loses detail)
- Using LUTs without proper primary correction first
- Inconsistent grade between shots in the same scene
